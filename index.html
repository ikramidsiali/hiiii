Learning plan



Learning a new language takes time and practice, so don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away. Keep practicing , be patient and you will gradually improve your English skills over time. Here are the steps I recommend you to follow in order to learn effectively.

1-Find your "why": Think of what drives you to learn English and remind yourself of it every time because that will fill you with energy to go after your goal.

2-Start with the basics: Begin with the basics of English vocabulary , pronunciation and grammar. This will create a strong foundation for further language development.

3-Practice listening and reading: Listen to English podcasts or songs , watch videos and reed books to see how the words and expressions you learned are used in daily life conversations. You will understand most of what you read or hear if you did the previous step and you can add up to what you have learned by searching for the meaning of the words that are new to you.

4-Practice speaking : You can practice speaking English every day with a native speaker or a language partner. You can also write in English and get feedback from someone who knows the language very well or AI.

Moreover , you can label the objects in your house so that you see them each day to remember them.

Varieties of English



English is a global language spoken in many different countries around the world with a number of regional dialects. Each variety of english has a different vocabulary, spelling and pronunciation.
The varieties of English with the largest number of native speakers are American English and British English. The vocabulary of these two has almost become the same since Britons are using more American words because they have been influenced by American films and songs.
The words in this website are spelled in American english and you might find some British words in the vocabulary part so that you could understand them if you hear people using them.
The other major varieties of English are Canadian English, Australian English, New Zealand English, South African English and Indian English. Here is a list of some words that are different in British and American english in case you want to look at the difference.

Grammar:

Proper use of grammar is essential for effective communication, as it helps to ensure clarity, accuracy, and coherence in written and spoken language. Here are the grammar lessons you need to study:

 

Parts of speech:

  

Every word in a sentence can be classified into one of the eight parts of speech: noun, pronoun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, conjunction, or interjection.


 

Capitalization:

  

Proper nouns, the first word in a sentence, and other specific words should be capitalized.


 

Reported speech:

  

lessons on how to report what someone else has said, including indirect speech and direct speech.


 

Active and passive voice:

  

Sentences can be written in either active or passive voice. In active voice, the subject performs the action, while in passive voice, the subject receives the action. For example, "The boy kicked the ball" is active voice, while "The ball was kicked by the boy" is passive voice.


 

Comparative and superlative forms:

  

Adjectives and adverbs have comparative and superlative forms to indicate degrees of comparison. For example, "big, bigger, biggest" or "well, better, best".


Relative clauses:

lessons on the use of relative clauses (which provide additional information about a noun or pronoun) and how to use them correctly.


Articles:

lessons on the use of articles (a, an, the) and when to use them in context.
